## The Seven Modes

All modes are derived from the major scale, starting on different degrees.

### Mode Overview (Using C Major as Parent)

| Mode | Starting Note | Formula | Character |
|------|---------------|---------|-----------|
| Ionian | C | W-W-H-W-W-W-H | Bright, happy (= Major) |
| Dorian | D | W-H-W-W-W-H-W | Minor with major 6th |
| Phrygian | E | H-W-W-W-H-W-W | Dark, Spanish |
| Lydian | F | W-W-W-H-W-W-H | Dreamy, floating |
| Mixolydian | G | W-W-H-W-W-H-W | Bluesy major |
| Aeolian | A | W-H-W-W-H-W-W | Sad (= Natural Minor) |
| Locrian | B | H-W-W-H-W-W-W | Unstable, rare |

(W = Whole step, H = Half step)

## Mode Characteristics

### Ionian (Major Scale)
```
C D E F G A B C
1 2 3 4 5 6 7 1
```
- **Mood**: Happy, bright, resolved
- **Use**: Pop, classical, children's music
- **Avoid**: When you want tension or sadness

### Dorian
```
D E F G A B C D
1 2 ♭3 4 5 6 ♭7 1
```
- **Mood**: Minor but hopeful, sophisticated
- **Use**: Jazz, funk, folk, minor grooves
- **Key feature**: Major 6th in a minor scale
- **Example songs**: "So What" (Miles Davis), "Scarborough Fair"

### Phrygian
```
E F G A B C D E
1 ♭2 ♭3 4 5 ♭6 ♭7 1
```
- **Mood**: Dark, exotic, Spanish/Middle Eastern
- **Use**: Flamenco, metal, film scores
- **Key feature**: ♭2 creates tension
- **Example**: Flamenco guitar, heavy metal riffs

### Lydian
```
F G A B C D E F
1 2 3 #4 5 6 7 1
```
- **Mood**: Dreamy, ethereal, floating
- **Use**: Film scores, jazz, progressive rock
- **Key feature**: #4 creates brightness
- **Example**: "Flying in a Blue Dream" (Joe Satriani)

### Mixolydian
```
G A B C D E F G
1 2 3 4 5 6 ♭7 1
```
- **Mood**: Bluesy, rock, unresolved major
- **Use**: Rock, blues, folk, funk
- **Key feature**: ♭7 adds blues feel
- **Example**: "Sweet Home Alabama", "Norwegian Wood"

### Aeolian (Natural Minor)
```
A B C D E F G A
1 2 ♭3 4 5 ♭6 ♭7 1
```
- **Mood**: Sad, melancholic, dark
- **Use**: Rock ballads, classical, pop
- **Example**: Most "sad" songs

### Locrian
```
B C D E F G A B
1 ♭2 ♭3 4 ♭5 ♭6 ♭7 1
```
- **Mood**: Extremely unstable, dissonant
- **Use**: Rarely used; metal, avant-garde
- **Key feature**: Diminished 5th (♭5)
- **Warning**: Very difficult to use musically

## Pentatonic Scales

### Major Pentatonic
```
C D E G A (C)
1 2 3 5 6
```
- **Mood**: Happy, open, folk-like
- **Use**: Country, pop, rock solos
- **Safe scale**: Almost impossible to play wrong notes

### Minor Pentatonic
```
A C D E G (A)
1 ♭3 4 5 ♭7
```
- **Mood**: Bluesy, rock
- **Use**: Blues, rock, pop solos
- **Most common** scale for guitar solos

## Blues Scale

### Minor Blues
```
A C D D# E G (A)
1 ♭3 4 ♭5 5 ♭7
```
- Minor pentatonic + "blue note" (♭5)
- **Mood**: Soulful, expressive
- **Use**: Blues, jazz, rock

### Major Blues
```
C D D# E G A (C)
1 2 ♭3 3 5 6
```
- Major pentatonic + ♭3
- **Mood**: Happy blues

## Other Useful Scales

### Harmonic Minor
```
A B C D E F G# A
1 2 ♭3 4 5 ♭6 7
```
- **Mood**: Classical, exotic, dramatic
- **Use**: Classical, metal, Middle Eastern
- **Key feature**: Augmented 2nd (F to G#)

### Melodic Minor (Ascending)
```
A B C D E F# G# A
1 2 ♭3 4 5 6 7
```
- **Mood**: Jazz, sophisticated minor
- **Use**: Jazz improvisation

### Whole Tone Scale
```
C D E F# G# A# (C)
1 2 3 #4 #5 #6
```
- **Mood**: Dreamy, floating, ambiguous
- **Use**: Impressionist music, transitions
- **Note**: No half steps, very unstable

### Chromatic Scale
```
C C# D D# E F F# G G# A A# B C
```
- All 12 notes
- **Use**: Passing tones, tension

## Mode Selection by Genre

| Genre | Recommended Modes |
|-------|-------------------|
| Pop (Happy) | Ionian, Mixolydian |
| Pop (Sad) | Aeolian, Dorian |
| Jazz | Dorian, Mixolydian, Lydian |
| Blues | Mixolydian, Minor Pentatonic |
| Rock | Aeolian, Dorian, Mixolydian |
| Metal | Phrygian, Aeolian, Locrian |
| Classical | Ionian, Aeolian, Harmonic Minor |
| Folk | Dorian, Mixolydian, Ionian |
| Electronic | Lydian, Phrygian, Whole Tone |
| Film (Epic) | Lydian, Phrygian |
| Film (Dark) | Phrygian, Locrian, Harmonic Minor |
