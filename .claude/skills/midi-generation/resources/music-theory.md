## Scales and Keys

### Major Scale Intervals
```
W - W - H - W - W - W - H
(W = Whole step, H = Half step)
```

### Common Keys and Their Notes

| Key | Notes |
|-----|-------|
| C Major | C D E F G A B |
| G Major | G A B C D E F# |
| D Major | D E F# G A B C# |
| A Major | A B C# D E F# G# |
| F Major | F G A B♭ C D E |
| B♭ Major | B♭ C D E♭ F G A |

### Minor Scale (Natural)
```
W - H - W - W - H - W - W
```

| Key | Notes |
|-----|-------|
| A minor | A B C D E F G |
| E minor | E F# G A B C D |
| D minor | D E F G A B♭ C |

## Cadences (Phrase Endings)

Cadences create resolution or tension at phrase endings.

| Cadence | Progression | Feel | Use |
|---------|-------------|------|-----|
| **Authentic** | V → I | Strong resolution | End of piece/section |
| **Half** | any → V | Incomplete, expectant | Mid-phrase pause |
| **Plagal** | IV → I | Soft, "Amen" | Gentle endings |
| **Deceptive** | V → vi | Surprising | Extend phrases |

### Examples in C Major

```
Authentic:  G → C   (strong ending)
Half:       C → G   (pause, expects more)
Plagal:     F → C   (soft ending)
Deceptive:  G → Am  (surprise, continues)
```

## Safe Note Combinations

### Consonant Intervals (sound stable)
- Unison (same note)
- Octave (8 semitones)
- Perfect Fifth (7 semitones)
- Perfect Fourth (5 semitones)
- Major Third (4 semitones)
- Minor Third (3 semitones)
- Major Sixth (9 semitones)
- Minor Sixth (8 semitones)

### Dissonant Intervals (use carefully)
- Minor Second (1 semitone) - **avoid**
- Major Second (2 semitones) - passing only
- Tritone (6 semitones) - resolve quickly
- Major Seventh (11 semitones) - jazz only

## Quick Reference: Notes to Avoid Playing Together

**These combinations create harsh dissonance:**

| Note 1 | Avoid with |
|--------|------------|
| C | C#, B |
| D | D#, C# |
| E | F, D# |
| F | F#, E |
| G | G#, F# |
| A | A#, G# |
| B | C, A# |

**Rule**: Avoid notes 1 semitone apart playing simultaneously.

## Recommended Chord Voicings

### For full sound without dissonance:

**Piano/Keys**: Spread notes across octaves
```
Left hand:  C2 - G2
Right hand: E4 - G4 - C5
```

**Ensemble**: Assign chord tones to different instruments
```
Bass:     C2 (root)
Strings:  E3, G3 (3rd, 5th)
Piano:    C4, E4, G4 (full chord)
```
